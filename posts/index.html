CTC v/s VTC
You might have heard about CTC
( Cost To Company )

But are you aware of your VTC ?

Before offering you a job, companies will often try to ask your expectations and then based on your current CTC, they will determine what you will be worth and then present you with the final numbers. You are then asked to either accept it or negotiate it further.

But I am creating a new term called VTC ( Value To Company ).
I'm talking about the value you will provide to the employer.

But who can determine your VTC and how ?

Nobody in the world can determine your VTC but yourself. And your current CTC has nothing to do with your VTC. You might be underpaid or overpaid ( depends ). But I'm trying to tell you that the intrinsic value of your VTC is an entity that can never be measured by an external entity.

So the next time you get into a CTC negotiation, always try to negotiate based on what you believe your VTC actually is.

I am deriving the below formula as a result.

Let the company's offer = o( CTC )
Let your absolute VTC = y( VTC )
Let the intrinsic value add = V

Now,

y( VTC ) = o( CTC ) + V

Always remember,
Nobody in the world can measure the intrinsic value of "V" but yourself. This intrinsic value keeps growing exponentially as you progress in your career and acquire new skills.

I could have just said it in plain English but I felt making it a mathematical expression will get your attention better 🙂


Asking for pay slips
Today I will address one of the sensitive things that are still being followed in few software companies - "Asking the previous employer payslips before sending offer letter"

There are 2 reasons for asking it.
  • To verify if the candidate is not faking experience.
  • To determine their hike.

I'm totally OK with the first one.

But the second reason separates "Great" companies from "OKish" companies.

Let me explain.

Companies already have certain baselines and slabs of CTC predefined for a given role. I understand negotiation is an art. But deciding the starting CTC based on previous employer salary is something we should reconsider whether to continue doing or not.

When you ask a selected candidate about his/her expectation, most candidates will say "Industry standard". This is because they value the company overall and believe that the standards are high and on par with the market.

After this, the moment previous employer payslips are asked, it could damage the reputation a bit.

Just a thought.
I'm just trying to express the impact of this sensitive thing.

3 tips for zoom interviews
With most companies moving to WFH model, interviews over video conferencing have become the only option lately. The world has changed and we software developers love "change" because we have been told that's the only "constant" in our career.

Below are 3 tips you must keep in mind before the interview starts. I assume you have prepared well before this day.

#1. Join the call on time and turn the webcam ON.
- By turning the webcam ON, you get immediately noticed as most candidates and interviewers will not turn it on. They will focus on you a bit more.

#2. Keep your favorite code editor handy.
- Few companies will prefer an online editor. But many times, the interviewer may ask you to open in your local machine due to VPN issues. Don't waste time installing it. Keep it ready.

#3. Keep a sample project ready.
- The last thing you want is getting stuck in setting up a sample project. Don't waste time installing dependencies. Keep the sample project ready where you can quickly code and show the output. If you are in a react.js interview, keep a" hello world" app ready with index.js

All the best 👍

The power of hooks
🤯 React's useEffect( ) is more powerful than I thought.

Many react developers got a taste of "hooks" last year and ever since, it has been hard to go back to class components. I mean, who wants those life cycle methods.. yukkk 😪

The very first hook that we learnt was obviously "useState ( )" which allowed us to maintain an internal state for a given react function component.

👨‍💻 const [ state, setState ] = useState(initialValue);

We loved ❤️ the performance it brought as redux state updates are comparatively slower. When I fire a redux action to update the state, I always felt the lag 👎 if I am using that state to render a certain UI component. ( Like opening and closing a Material UI drawer at a rapid rate. Browser freezes after a while ). The last thing I want on my website is a browser freeze 😡

But it's always fast with the component's local state 😇

The second hook we learnt is useEffect ( );

useEffect(() => {
// Your code
}, [variables]);

Turns out, this is not just for executing some side effect code. It can control the app UX with so much minimal code 😲

Future of interviews
Does the future look bright with the interview process followed by most of the software companies today?

Well, maybe...but can there be a better way? let's find out.

I have been interviewing candidates for the past 4 years in various software companies for various roles. And just like you, I have also given interviews as a candidate which got me the jobs I wanted in my career till now.

But I somehow feel the process is getting old school.

The same old DS, Algo & Problem solving rounds, the same old one-sided interrogation trying to measure a candidate.

I think times are changing and machines are becoming powerful, our life is getting more organized than ever. Systems are getting more efficient than ever before. And I see that human psychology also has changed a lot. We don't talk too much like we used to and we have found various ways to get things done fast with minimal interaction.

Let me give you 1 example.
Consider an interviewer who is confident to interview a candidate in a technical round. Do you think he/she will have the same confidence if the seats are swapped?

I bet the answer is No.

So definitely we need different measuring sticks in the future. We should find out the hidden talent and not try to put pressure for 45 mins and judge based on that.

LinkedIn job referral
We all are looking for opportunities and one of the best ways to get one is to "ask". Yes, "asking" is powerful but we should understand how to get things done as well.

I'm giving you the Do's ✅ and Dont's ❌ while asking a job referral from your LinkedIn connection. Or if you don't have a connection, earning one as well.

The Dont's: ❌
Remember that you need something and not them. So please don't just send a 1 liner message like "can you refer me ?". Also, don't start the conversation with such lines. Remember that they are also employees like you and they have priorities. Don't directly send the Job ID without even introducing yourself or mentioning your past accomplishments/ current skills.

The Do's: ✅
Always start with a greeting and respect their time. Just ask how was their day and if they are open for a quick chat. If you have seen some of their content on LinkedIn, acknowledge it. After that instead of sending a huge message with Job ID, start introducing yourself and mention that you are interested in their organisation. Then explain a little bit about your past accomplishments.

Now is your chance to search online and find the Job ID.
Also send a URL link of the JD so that it's easy for them.

All the best guys. 👍
